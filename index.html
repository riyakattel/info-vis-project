Project Write-up

Three Visualization Tasks

  1. Relationship and outlier task: Identify whether shoppers with higher income also spend more per trip and detect unusual customers with exceptionally high or low spending.
  2. Comparison task: Compare average purchase behavior across grocery chains to see which chains attract higher-spending customers.
  3. Distribution task: Examine how purchase amount varies across payment methods and whether some methods show wider spread or more outliers.

Visual Representations and Why

  • Scatter plot for Income vs PurchaseAmount because it reveals correlation, density, clusters, and outliers in two quantitative attributes.
  • Bar chart for average PurchaseAmount by Chain because chain is categorical and average spending is easy to compare with aligned bars.
  • Box plot for PurchaseAmount by PaymentMethod because it summarizes distribution, median, quartiles, spread, and outliers better than a pie chart or simple counts.

Interaction Design

  • Brushing on the scatter plot to select a subset of shoppers and coordinate updates in the bar and box plots.
  • Click filtering on the bar chart to filter all views to a selected store chain.
  • Dropdown filters for gender, occupation, and payment method, plus an age slider for range filtering.
  • Hover tooltips for detailed values on marks.
  • Reset button to remove all active interactions quickly.

Coordination

All views are linked. A brush selection in the scatter plot updates the bar chart, box plot, and summary cards. Clicking a chain bar filters the scatter and box views. Global controls affect every chart simultaneously.
